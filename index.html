About

My name is Chez'lene Cornwall. I have been programming since I was a senior in high school. I decided to further my education and go to college for an associate's degree specializing in software development. A career in software has been a long-time goal of mine, but freelancing has been something I have been interested in very recently. I like the idea of being able to work on multiple projects at once and gain experience more rapidly by working with various clients. I feel that I can truly push myself through this career path.


The first programming language I learned was Python and it is also the language I am the most comfortable with. I credit it as one of the main reasons I am still a programmer today. While in high school, I also learned the basics of Javascript. I had the opportunity of interning with a local business' IT department and was given the task of developing a desktop application that could be used to generate business contracts on Microsoft Word and budget spreadsheets in Excel. After completing this assignment, I was given another task to retrieve the data from the company's solar panels and populate a CSV file. In my college experience, I, along with my group members, had the opportunity to create a another desktop application, this time using Python and MySql. We created an inventory system where users could control the inventory and users with in the system.
